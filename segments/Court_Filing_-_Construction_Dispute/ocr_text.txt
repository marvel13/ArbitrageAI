Plaintiff submits that the date of commencement of work was fixed
as 18.07.2018, and the work was required to be completed within
30 months from the expiry of 15 days from the date of the Work
Order, i.e., on or before 17.01.2021. The 15 days' period was granted

It is pertinent to note here that the tender submitted on 13.10.2017
was allowed to be acted upon only in July 2018, i.e., after a delayed
period of 9 months by the Defendant. During this period, the prices
of various construction materials had increased far beyond the
anticipated range, and several terms and conditions of the contract
had also undergone significant changes. Notwithstanding this, the
Plaintiff commenced the work and proceeded with due diligence
under the contract, which in any event does not curtail or extinguish
the legal rights of the contractor. Although Clause 10 C (ii) provides
for price variation based on wholesale price indices drawn from all-
India data, the actual prevailing market rates in Bengaluru had
escalated abnormally and were not adequately reflected by such
indices.
