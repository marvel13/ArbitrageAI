As the global impact of the outbreak worsens, causing disruption to international trade and
other commercial activities, there is risk of Contractor being unable to perform the contractual
obligations. Further, the Govt of India has also directed various organizations / Corporates, that,
during this period of unprecedented crises, they shall not deduct the wages of their Employees/staff
/labouretc., for their absence or to lay them off. This imposes on the Contractor, the burden of the
cost of all resources mobilized on the Project.
In this context, the project, being manpower intensive activity, URC is working at subject
project site with around 430 workmen of different cadre and necessary heavy equipment, plant and
machineries, centering and shuttering materials, perishable, non perishable, technical and non-
technical staffs, financial charges, medical and other expenses etc., which has been due to the
lockdown /restrictions imposed by the Government. At this juncture this letter to may be treated
as intimation as per Contract.
The actual consequence of such suspension in terms of time and cost and the recommencement of
works under the Project shall be addressed as the situation becomes normal and the restrictions are
revoked by the Government.
