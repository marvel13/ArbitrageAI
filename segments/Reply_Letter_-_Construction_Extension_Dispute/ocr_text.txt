Reply: All the hindrances claimed by you vide letter no. URC/ISRO AITF-1 Extn/2022/1057
dated 09/09/2022 were scrutinised in detail as per the agreement conditions. By considering
the overlap of hindrances based on its actual occurrences, the final extension of time approval
was accorded with justified delays of 415 days and unjustified delays of 68 days. The delays
due to hindrances which are beyond the control of contractor has been considered and
accordingly, the period of 415 days from 18/01/2021 to 08/03/2022 is considered as justified
delay period.
As per clause 15 of agreement conditions,
Quote 'The contractor shall suspend the execution of work or any part or parts thereof,
wherever called upon in writing by the Engineer-in-charge to do so and shall not resume
work thereon until so directed in writing by him. The Contractor will be allowed an extension
of time for completion not less than the period of suspension but no other claims in this
respect for compensation or otherwise, however will be admissible. ' Unquote.
Accordingly, the temporary suspension period due to COVID-19 pandemic and the
force majeure period is considered only for extension of time and not for benefit of
escalation. Thereby, the benefit of escalation is provided for contract period plus an
additional period of 75 days within the justified delay period.
